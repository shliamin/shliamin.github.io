I am a Computer Scientist with a Bachelor of Science in Computer Science from the University of Applied Sciences Berlin. From 2015 to 2019, I studied Electrical Engineering and IT Security at Ruhr University Bochum. Since 2019, I have been engaged in full stack development and data science. I have completed three internships at IT companies and have received recommendation letters from Rhenus Logistics, the Data Centre of the University of Applied Sciences Berlin, and Mercedes-Benz. Additionally, I have been freelancing for several years. I author articles on technology and innovation in IT, which you can read on my Medium profile by clicking on the respective icons below my photo. I am always committed to lifelong learning: MERN, Spring.

What matters to me is solving business, scientific, and social problems through technological solutions. I believe this is the foundation for the development of individuals, companies, and society as a whole.

To align with my values, I create software that helps achieve these goals. Besides developing software, I write about my processes and frequently share open source code. Examples of my work can be found in the My Work section.

My goal is to create IT solutions that directly impact the future development of individuals, companies, and society. By making our lives more automated and efficient, maintaining competitive advantages, and understanding and leveraging data effectively, we can make high-quality insights and decisions. This belief drives my work.

My clients include technology companies and institutes focused on research, innovation, production improvement, and developing proprietary technologies to gain a competitive edge. I freelance and often help non-IT professionals solve their technological problems as well.
